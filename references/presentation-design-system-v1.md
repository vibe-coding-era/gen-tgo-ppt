## Universal Quality Bar

Act as a world-class presentation designer with the taste level of Apple Keynote, OpenAI, McKinsey, Tesla AI Day, Google I/O, Netflix, and Palantir.

Requirements:

- Keep a unified design language.
- Give every page one core claim.
- Prefer visual structures over prose.
- Avoid long text blocks.
- Strengthen title design.
- Strengthen data visualization.
- Support 16:9 widescreen and 4K presentation use.
- Include cover, chapter, content, data, and summary pages when the deck scope calls for them.
- Avoid generic templates, domestic training-course style, and filler decorations.
- Aim for magazine-level layout, cinematic visual weight, and product-keynote polish.

## Style 1: Executive Board

Use for CEO reports, board meetings, enterprise strategy, annual planning, and investment committees.

- Keywords: professional, credible, restrained, rational, serious, strategic, decision-oriented.
- Visuals: deep blue, business gray, white, large whitespace, data-first charts, very few decorative elements.
- Reference mood: McKinsey, BCG, Bain, Fortune 500 board reporting.
- TGO/GTLC handling: use white or light templates; preserve top-right logo/footer; prefer clean charts and decision tables.

## Style 2: Silicon Valley

Use for fundraising, AI startups, product launches, and investor roadshows.

- Keywords: growth, innovation, future, capital, speed, disruption.
- Visuals: huge titles, minimal content, one claim per page, large numbers, strong modern rhythm.
- Reference mood: Airbnb, Stripe, OpenAI, Y Combinator, Sequoia.
- TGO/GTLC handling: use light pages for readability and dark/cover pages for bold claims; keep copy extremely short.

## Style 3: Big Tech Keynote

Use for AI conferences, technical conferences, product launches, and major announcements.

- Keywords: futuristic, premium, breakthrough, innovation.
- Visuals: black or deep-blue backgrounds, immersive visuals, full-screen imagery, huge numbers, huge titles, minimal text.
- Reference mood: Apple Keynote, Tesla AI Day, Google I/O.
- TGO/GTLC handling: use dark template for cover, chapter, and keynote pages; use sparse copy and strong visual pacing.

## Style 4: AI Native

Recommended for Agent, AI Coding, Multi-Agent, AI Native enterprise, LLM, and AI organization topics.

- Keywords: agents, collaboration, future organization, intelligent networks, autonomous execution, digital employees.
- Visuals: dark background, blue-purple gradients, glow, neural networks, particle connections, future operating system, agent workflows.
- Reference mood: OpenAI, Anthropic, Cursor, Perplexity.
- TGO/GTLC handling: preserve TGO/GTLC dark backgrounds and add restrained glow/network visuals only when they encode workflow or hierarchy.

## Style 5: Data Intelligence

Use for benchmarks, AI Coding evaluation, business analysis, operations analysis, and growth analysis.

- Keywords: data, analysis, insight, growth, efficiency, metrics.
- Visuals: charts occupy at least 60% of the slide, dashboard logic, data-driven layout, rigorous hierarchy.
- Reference mood: Palantir, Snowflake, Databricks.
- TGO/GTLC handling: use white/light templates for dense data; keep chart geometry editable and legible.

## Style 6: Minimal Luxury

Use for founder speeches, brand strategy, high-end forums, and company culture.

- Keywords: premium, restrained, elegant, timeless, luxury.
- Visuals: black/white/gray, extreme whitespace, very few elements, typography-driven, editorial magazine feel.
- Reference mood: Apple, Hermes, LVMH.
- TGO/GTLC handling: use blank or sparse layouts; preserve brand chrome but reduce text and decoration.

## Style 7: Chinese Business

Use for SOEs, central enterprises, government projects, industrial upgrading, and China technology narratives.

- Keywords: oriental aesthetics, cultural confidence, China technology, industrial upgrading.
- Visuals: whitespace, modern ink, red/gold accents, oriental geometry, traditional and technological fusion.
- TGO/GTLC handling: use TGO/GTLC frames as the base and add Chinese-style accents only if they support the content.

## Style 8: Creative Festival

Use for AI comics/drama, innovation conferences, design conferences, community events, creator conferences.

- Keywords: creative, young, energetic, breakthrough, entertainment, future culture.
- Visuals: high-saturation contrast, bold typography, visual experiments, illustration/comic elements, motion-graphic feel.
- Reference mood: SXSW, Adobe MAX, Netflix creative events.
- TGO/GTLC handling: keep brand background, but allow stronger accent colors and expressive layouts on creative sections.

## Mixed Style Rules

- Let cover/chapter pages carry stronger styles than content pages.
- Use Style 3 or 4 for opening and AI chapters, Style 1 or 5 for decision/data pages, Style 8 for creative sections, and Style 6 for founder/keynote reflection pages.
- If a mixed style risks visual fragmentation, keep the same TGO/GTLC background system, typography scale, footer/logo placement, and page rhythm.
- Record the selected style in the deck plan for every slide.
